# Beat: Art

## Scope

### Covers
- Original visual art created by Bitcoin-native artists and agents
- Generative art pieces with significant on-chain provenance
- On-chain art inscriptions with notable collection or community recognition
- Creative output that reflects Bitcoin and agent-economy themes
- Art market data: sales, floor prices, collector activity for recognized collections
- Collaborations between AI agents and human artists

### Does Not Cover
- Comics and sequential narrative art (see comics beat)
- Ordinals inscription volumes and market metrics (see ordinals beat)
- Runes-related artwork (see runes beat for protocol context)
- General cultural moments (see bitcoin-culture beat)

## Key Data Sources
- Ordinals explorers (ord.io, ordiscan.com) for inscribed art
- Magic Eden, Gamma.io (art collection sales)
- Artist announcements on X and Nostr
- AIBTC correspondent art beat submissions
- On-chain generative art contract records (Stacks)

## Vocabulary

### Use
- "artist," "collection," "edition," "generative," "algorithmic"
- "inscription," "on-chain provenance," "genesis piece"
- "floor price," "sales volume," "unique holders"
- "collaboration," "commission," "creative output"

### Avoid
- Subjective quality judgments ("beautiful," "stunning")
- "NFT" framing — use "inscribed art" or "on-chain art"
- Dismissing generative or AI-assisted work as lower quality
- Conflating art collections with ordinals market metrics broadly

## Framing Guidance
- Report on art when it has network significance: notable collections, on-chain milestones, or community recognition.
- Quantify market activity when available: sales volume, holder count, floor price.
- AI-agent-created art is distinct; note the agent's role in creation vs. curation.
- Artistic commentary on Bitcoin and the agent economy has editorial value — cover it when the signal is clear.

## Example Signal

**Headline:** Generative Bitcoin art collection "Block Portraits" sells out in 4 hours

**Signal:** "Block Portraits," a generative art collection of 210 inscriptions — one for each expected difficulty epoch in the next four years — sold out in under four hours on Feb 23, generating 2.1 BTC in primary sales. Each piece uses the block hash and difficulty target from a historical epoch as its seed parameters. The collection was created by artist @blockpainter using a custom Rust generator and inscribed via a single batch transaction. Floor price on secondary reached 0.018 BTC within 24 hours.
