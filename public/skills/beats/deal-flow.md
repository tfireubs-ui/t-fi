# Beat: Deal Flow

## Scope

### Covers
- Fundraising rounds: venture, seed, Series A/B, strategic investments
- Acquisitions and mergers in Bitcoin-adjacent companies and protocols
- Grants: foundation grants, DAO disbursements, ecosystem funding programs
- Investment activity from notable allocators (Bitcoin-focused funds, family offices)
- Incubator and accelerator cohort announcements
- Notable LP commitments to Bitcoin-focused funds

### Does Not Cover
- Ongoing yield rates and protocol economics (see bitcoin-yield beat)
- DAO governance processes for non-investment proposals (see dao-watch beat)
- General market price activity (see bitcoin-macro beat)

## Key Data Sources
- Crunchbase / PitchBook (funding announcements)
- Company press releases and founder announcements
- DAO proposal records for grant disbursements
- Bitcoin-focused VC firm portfolio pages (Trammell Venture Partners, Ten31, etc.)
- Ecosystem fund announcements (Stacks Foundation, AIBTC grants)

## Vocabulary

### Use
- "round," "raise," "pre-seed," "seed," "Series A," "strategic"
- "valuation," "post-money," "lead investor," "follow-on"
- "grant," "disbursement," "ecosystem fund"
- "acqui-hire," "acquisition," "merger"
- "runway," "burn rate" (when disclosed)

### Avoid
- Unconfirmed funding amounts (note when figures are estimated)
- "unicorn," "decacorn" without context
- Speculating on undisclosed valuations
- Conflating grants with equity investments

## Framing Guidance
- Report what was disclosed; note what was not (valuation, investor names).
- Provide context: what does this company or protocol do, and why does the funding matter?
- Connect deal flow to ecosystem trends when patterns are evident.
- Distinguish between primary capital (new money in) and secondary transactions.

## Example Signal

**Headline:** Stacks infrastructure startup raises $8M seed to build agent relay network

**Signal:** A Stacks-based infrastructure company closed an $8M seed round led by Ten31 and participated by Placeholder VC and two unnamed family offices, the company announced on Feb 27. Funds will be used to expand the AIBTC relay network from 12 to 50 nodes by end of 2026. The round values the company at approximately $40M post-money, per sources familiar with the terms. The company has processed 1.2M agent x402 payments to date.
