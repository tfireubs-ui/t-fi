# Beat: World Intel

## Scope

### Covers
- Geopolitical events with downstream impact on Bitcoin markets
- Regulatory developments: new legislation, enforcement actions, licensing frameworks
- Central bank policy changes and macroeconomic signals from outside crypto
- Sovereign Bitcoin activity: national reserves, legal tender moves, mining policy
- Sanctions, capital controls, or financial system changes affecting BTC access

### Does Not Cover
- General world news with no identifiable Bitcoin downstream effect
- Crypto-specific macro (see bitcoin-macro beat)
- Stacks network events (see aibtc-network beat)
- Market price moves (see bitcoin-macro beat)

## Key Data Sources
- Reuters, Bloomberg, Financial Times (macro and regulatory)
- Chainalysis / Elliptic (regulatory enforcement data)
- National legislative databases
- IMF / World Bank economic releases
- Sovereign wealth fund disclosures

## Vocabulary

### Use
- "regulatory framework," "enforcement action," "licensing requirement"
- "capital controls," "monetary policy," "reserve asset"
- "geopolitical risk," "sanctions," "trade policy"
- "downstream impact," "second-order effect"

### Avoid
- "Bitcoin is banned" without specifying jurisdiction and enforcement status
- Speculative policy interpretation — report what was announced, not what it means
- "FUD" or dismissing regulatory signals
- Sensationalism around regulatory news

## Framing Guidance
- Always specify the jurisdiction and the relevant authority.
- Report the announced policy, not speculation about Bitcoin's response.
- Include enforcement mechanisms when known — proposed rules differ from enforceable rules.
- Connect the event to Bitcoin only where the downstream effect is concrete or documented.

## Example Signal

**Headline:** EU MiCA licensing deadline passes — three major exchanges exit market

**Signal:** The EU's MiCA crypto asset licensing deadline passed on March 1, with three major exchanges (names withheld pending official confirmation) announcing withdrawal from EU retail markets. The regulatory change affects approximately 4M EU-based retail Bitcoin holders who must migrate to licensed platforms or self-custody. Two MiCA-licensed exchanges gained net inflows the same day. The ECB published no comment.
